Our mission

Member countries of the United Nations adopted a set of goals in order to achieve gender equality, empower women and stop any kind of violence against women and girls.

We would like to support these goals by making data and knowledge accessible for data scientists, statisticians, journalists, students and anyone who is interested in conducting research to help women in need. We believe that data analysis can uncover interesting and valuable connections that can help governments, non-profits and decision makers to understand women issues more deeply. Therefore we curated a library of freely available research papers and databases that you can use for your project.

Research papers

Browse our library of freely available scientific studies on gender equality, empowerment of women and violence against women and girls. We collected academic papers from many different reliable online sources. Feel free to download and read any of them!

Women’s and men’s reports of past-year prevalence of intimate partner violence...

- Rachel Jewkes, et al.

The study uses data from 4 countries in Asia and the Pacific to compare the population prevalence rates of past-year IPV and nonpartner rape from women’s and men’s reports and present an analysis of drivers of women’s experience of past-year physical or sexual IPV.

Why Do Some Men Use Violence Against Women and How Can We Prevent It?...

- Emma Fulu, et al.

The report was conducted in Bangladesh, Cambodia, China, Indonesia, Sri Lanka and Papua New Guinea. It explores the prevalence of men’s use of violence against women in the survey sites, and shows what factors make men more or less likely to use violence.

Intimate partner violence and women's physical and mental health in the WHO multi...

- Mary Ellsberg, et al.

This article summarises findings from ten countries from the WHO multi-country study on women's health and domestic violence against women. Women aged 15–49 years were interviewed about their experiences of physically and sexually violent acts by a current or former intimate male partner.
